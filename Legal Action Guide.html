Track 1 — DOL / Wage and Hour Division (FMLA Complaint)

What It Is

The U.S. Department of Labor’s Wage and Hour Division (WHD) enforces the FMLA. Filing a complaint is free, does not require an attorney, and triggers a federal investigation. If WHD finds a violation, they can order your employer to pay back wages, liquidated damages (double the back pay), and even reinstatement.

What You Can Recover

  • Lost wages and benefits from the date of termination
  • Liquidated damages equal to the lost wages (doubles the amount) — if the violation was willful
  • Attorney fees and court costs (if litigation follows)
  • Reinstatement to your former position (optional — you may decline)

How to File

Step 1. Go to dol.gov/agencies/whd/contact/complaints
Step 2. Click "File a Complaint Online" — you will fill out a form describing the violation.
Step 3. Alternatively, call the WHD toll-free: 1-866-487-9243
Step 4. Or visit the Houston WHD District Office in person:
U.S. Department of Labor — WHD Houston District Office
525 S. Griffin St., Suite 800, Dallas TX 75202 (regional)
Or: 8701 S. Gessner Dr., Suite 910, Houston, TX 77074 — call 713-339-5500 to confirm address
Step 5. Bring or attach your supporting documents (the files in your Supporting Documents folder).

Track 2 — EEOC Charge (ADA / Disability Discrimination)

What It Is and Why It May Apply to You

The Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) handles workplace discrimination based on disability (Americans with Disabilities Act), among other protected categories. Because your FMLA leave was for a serious health condition, that same condition may qualify as a disability under the ADA. If so, Centene may have also violated the ADA by: (1) failing to provide reasonable accommodation; (2) terminating you in connection with a disability; or (3) treating your medical absences differently than non-disabled employees.

You must file an EEOC charge before you can sue under the ADA in federal court. In Texas, the deadline is 300 days from the discriminatory act (approximately September 16, 2026 based on a November 20 termination). This is the most time-sensitive deadline you have.

How to File

Step 1. Go to publicportal.eeoc.gov — you can begin the process entirely online.
Step 2. Select "I want to file a charge of discrimination."
Step 3. You will be asked intake questions. Key answers:
— Basis of discrimination: Disability (ADA) — your FMLA leave was for a serious health condition
— Type of discrimination: Termination / Discharge
— Employer: Centene Corporation, remote (Houston, TX)
Step 4. The EEOC will schedule an intake interview. Bring all your documentation.
Step 5. Houston EEOC District Office (if filing in person):
1919 Smith St., 7th Floor, Houston, TX 77002
Phone: 1-800-669-4000

Track 3 — Private Employment Attorney

Why You Should Contact One Now

Your case has strong documented evidence. Many employment attorneys take FMLA and ADA cases on contingency — meaning no upfront cost. They are paid only if you win or settle. An attorney can:

  • File the EEOC charge on your behalf (ensuring it is worded to preserve all legal claims)
  • Send a demand letter to Centene that may prompt a settlement before any litigation
  • Advise on the strength of a private federal lawsuit (which can yield back pay, liquidated damages, and attorney fees)
  • Navigate the interaction between your TWC appeal, DOL complaint, and any lawsuit

What to Look For

  • Employment attorneys in Houston who specialize in FMLA, ADA, or wrongful termination
  • Search: Texas Employment Lawyers Association (tela.org) or the National Employment Law Project (nelp.org)
  • Many offer free 30-minute consultations — contact 3–5 firms

Steps

Step 1. Open the attorney letter (Letters/Letter_1_Attorney_Inquiry.html) and customize with the firm name.
Step 2. Email or mail to 3–5 employment law firms in Houston. Most respond within a few days.
Step 3. At the consultation, bring: the written statement, the Lincoln Financial acknowledgement, and the supervisor occurrence email. Those three documents tell the whole story.
Step 4. Ask specifically: "Do I have an FMLA interference claim? An ADA claim? What are my damages?"
