Reason for Appeal

I believe my termination was the result of an improper application of attendance points that included absences protected under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA). Specifically, two attendance occurrences dated November 13 and November 18 were counted against me despite both being approved FMLA leave days. These absences were properly reported and documented through the company’s leave administrator, and they appear on my official leave records as FMLA-approved. Despite this, my employer treated those dates as attendance violations and included them in my total point count. As a result, I exceeded the company’s attendance threshold and was terminated. Had these FMLA-protected absences been correctly excluded as required under federal law, I would not have reached the attendance limit and would not have been terminated. I believe this constitutes interference with my FMLA rights and improper consideration of protected medical leave in an adverse employment decision.
